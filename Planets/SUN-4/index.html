CHAPTER 4: THE SUN AND IT'S LIFE CYCLE

The Main Sequence Phase

The Sun is currently in the main sequence phase of its life cycle, where it has spent about 4.6 billion years. During this stage, the Sun steadily fuses hydrogen into helium in its core, maintaining a balance between gravitational collapse and outward pressure from fusion. This phase is the longest in the life of a star, and our Sun is expected to remain in this stable condition for another 5 billion years.

Future Evolution

As the Sun exhausts its hydrogen fuel, it will undergo significant changes. In approximately 5 billion years, it will enter the red giant phase. The core will contract and heat up, while the outer layers will expand and cool, causing the Sun to grow in size and engulf the inner planets, including possibly Earth. During this phase, helium fusion will occur, producing heavier elements like carbon and oxygen.

The End of the Sun

After the red giant phase, the Sun will shed its outer layers, creating a planetary nebula. The core will be left behind as a white dwarf, a dense, slowly cooling remnant about the size of Earth but containing a significant portion of the Sun's mass. This white dwarf will gradually cool over billions of years, eventually becoming a black dwarf, effectively marking the end of the Sun's life cycle.
